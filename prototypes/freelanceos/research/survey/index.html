How freelancers manage income, payments & uncertainty

Eight in-depth responses from Israeli freelancers and small-business owners - translated from Hebrew - on what they track each month, which tools they use, and where the friction lives.

8 responses June 2026 1:1 survey

Key insights

Patterns that came up again and again - useful anchors for product direction.

01

Chasing payments drains energy

Reminders, delayed transfers, and not knowing whether money arrived - the emotional cost often matters as much as timing.

02

Cash flow beats raw revenue

Expected vs. paid, pending invoices, balance, and net after tax - not just “how much did I make.”

03

Tools are fragmented

Invoicing, bank apps, accountants, Excel, email, and notes each hold part of the picture. Nothing gives one calm view.

04

Manual steps feel outdated

Receipts by hand, Excel exports, and reconciling sessions with payments were called slow - even when the tool was otherwise liked.

05

Uncertainty has many layers

Pipeline, national instability, taxes, and tasks outside their craft (marketing, admin) all add pressure.

06

The dream: automatic, synced, gentle

Auto receipts, calendar sync, smart reminders, and one place for income and tax - without becoming an accountant.

Survey questions

The prompts participants answered - shown here so responses below are easier to read in context.

Freelancer Payments & Earnings Survey

I'm exploring how freelancers manage payments, invoices, and earnings visibility. This should take about 5 minutes.

  1. 01

    Walk me through the last time you got paid by a client.

    What happened from completing the work until the money reached your account? Were there any frustrating or time-consuming steps?

  2. 02

    What financial information do you check most often during a typical month?

    For example: upcoming payments, overdue invoices, monthly earnings, cash flow, taxes, client profitability, account balance, etc.

  3. 03

    What causes you the most stress or uncertainty around your freelance income?

    What's the thing you find yourself thinking about or checking repeatedly?

  4. 04

    What tools do you currently use to manage payments and earnings?

    Spreadsheets, invoicing software, banking apps, accounting tools, notes, email, etc. What works well, and what feels painful or inefficient?

  5. 05

    If you could magically solve one problem related to getting paid, tracking income, or managing clients, what would it be?

    Why would that make the biggest difference to your business?
